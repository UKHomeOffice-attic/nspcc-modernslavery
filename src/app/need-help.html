HELP IS HERE

If you think you are, or have been, a victim of human trafficking, you can speak to someone from any of the organisations listed below. You can also find out more about slavery and how you can help fight the issue on these organisations’ websites.

ANTI-SLAVERY INTERNATIONAL

Founded in 1839, Anti-Slavery International is the oldest human rights organisation that works at local, national and international levels to eliminate all forms of slavery around the world.

BARNARDO’S

Our purpose as a charity today is to transform the lives of the UK’s most vulnerable children.

BCHA

We are a specialist housing and social care support provider, offering a wide range of help to people in vulnerable circumstances and to professionals trying to equip those in need. We are based in the South of England.

CITY HEARTS

City Hearts exists to provide hope to the most vulnerable, with the goal of loving them back to life. We are part of the national support provision helping victims of human trafficking.

CRIMESTOPPERS

If you have information to share on modern slavery, or any other crime, and wish to remain anonymous, you can contact the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or visit crimestoppers-uk.org. Your call or online form will not be traced and you will not have to go to court or give a statement to the police.

EAVES

Eaves Poppy Project provides high-quality support, advocacy and accommodation to women who have been trafficked to and within the UK.

ECPAT UK

ECPAT UK provides specialist safeguarding training to practitioners to identify trafficking and abuse, and works directly to support child victims.

GANGMASTERS LICENSING AUTHORITY

The Gangmasters Licensing Authority (GLA) regulates gangmasters who supply temporary workers in the agricultural and shellfish gathering industry, to prevent the exploitation of workers, and tackle related unlicensed/criminal activity.

THE MEDAILLE TRUST

The Medaille Trust is a charity founded to help women, men and children who have been freed from human-trafficking.

MIGRANT HELP

We provide migrants in the UK with the resources and support they need to find safety, access appropriate services and information and develop greater independence.

NSPCC

The NSPCC protects children across the UK. We run a wide range of services for both children and adults, including national helplines and the Child Trafficking Advice Centre (CTAC).

PALM COVE SOCIETY

Support & Accommodation for eligible victims of Trafficking, Forced Marriage, HBV, FGM(C), Refugees, Destitute Asylum Seekers and Migrants.

THE CHILDREN’S SOCIETY

We run specialist services for refugee and migrant children and children who have been victims of sexual exploitation, including victims of trafficking.

THE SALVATION ARMY

The Salvation Army is an international Christian church and charity with responsibility for delivering the UK government’s contract for support services for adult victims of Human Trafficking.

UNSEEN

Unseen tackles modern slavery by: providing safe refuge for survivors; training frontline services to spot the signs of human trafficking; and shaping government policy to prevent it happening in the future.

COMMUNITY SAFETY GLASGOW

TARA (Trafficking Awareness Raising Alliance) The TARA Service, provides advice and support to women, over the age of 18 yrs who have been trafficked for commercial sexual exploitation.

POLICE SCOTLAND

Keeping people safe.

BAWSO

BAWSO is a Welsh organisation delivering specialist services to people from Black and Ethnic Minority backgrounds who are affected by domestic abuse and other forms of abuse, including Female Genital Mutilation, Forced Marriage, Human Trafficking & Prostitution.

NEW PATHWAYS

We now offer a range of counselling, advocacy and support services for women, men, children and young people who are victims of rape or sexual abuse.

SAFER WALES

The people at Safer Wales work to help people feel safer and improve the life of our communities in Wales.

GATEWAY SERVICE FOR CHILDREN’S SOCIAL WORK

The Northern Ireland Health and Social Care (HSC) Trusts Gateway Services for Children’s Social Work are responsible for the provision of support and care to child victims of human trafficking.

WOMEN’S AID

Women’s Aid provides 24 hour accommodation, emotional and practical support through a network of refuges to women who are trafficked.
