What is this policy for?

This page (together with the above linked to privacy policy, our terms of use for this site and our Cookies Policy) set out or refer you to the basis on which the NSPCC or Home Office will process any personal information collected from you or provided by you in connection with this website.

2.0

WHOSE WEBSITE IS THIS?

This website is funded by the Home Office. It is operated, managed and hosted by the National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children (NSPCC). The Home Office and the NSPCC are committed to protecting your personal information.

3.0

WHAT WILL THE HOME OFFICE DO WITH ANY PERSONAL INFORMATION IT RECEIVES?

In the event that the Home Office receives any personal information, it will handle it in accordance with its standard privacy policy for all gov.uk websites, set out at https://www.gov.uk/help/privacy-policy/.

Any questions regarding what the Home Office does with personal information, or in relation to this website should be sent to the Data Protection Manager at Home Office, 2 Marsham Street, London, SW1P 4DF.

4.0

HOW ARE THE NSPCC INVOLVED?

The NSPCC operates, manages and hosts the website on behalf of the Home Office, and the Home Office has agreed that the NSPCC will receive all information that is submitted via the online form on the website, or by phone call or text message arising in connection with this website.

The NSPCC is a UK charity, with registered charity numbers 216401 in England and Wales and SC037717 in Scotland.

Any questions regarding the NSPCC’s use of personal information they receive from this website should be sent to the Data Protection Manager at NSPCC National Centre, Weston House, 42 Curtain Road, London EC2A 3NH or by email to dataprotectionoffice@nspcc.org.uk.

5.0

WHAT WILL HAPPEN TO PERSONAL INFORMATION I PROVIDE IN CONNECTION WITH THE WEBSITE?

Any information received via the phone may be recorded by the NSPCC; in addition there may be a supervisor listening to the call for security or quality assurance purposes.

If someone provides personal information via the online form, phone or any other way the NSPCC will not share that personal information with anyone unless it has permission, or in exceptional cases where required to do so by law. The NSPCC will store that information but will delete it within any timeframes required by legislation.

If someone provides any information that concerns child or adult protection or probable criminal activity regarding slavery and trafficking, the NSPCC will share that information, plus (if permission given) the name and contact details of the person that provided the information, with the Home Office and/or the police. When doing so NSPCC might state the following in relation to the person that provided the information:

  • the UK local authority they are calling from;
  • where they found the Modern Slavery phone number, email address or online form;
  • their preferred language and the preferred language of the person they are calling about;
  • their ethnicity and the ethnicity of the person they are calling about;
  • their gender and the gender of the person they are calling about;
  • their age and the age of the person they are calling about;
  • their primary concern e.g. slavery; and any sub-concern e.g. trafficking, sexual exploitation, labour, forced labour, domestic servitude or organ harvesting;
  • how long they have been concerned;
  • how long they believe the person they are calling about has been exploited;
  • their relationship with the person they are calling about;
  • their nationality and the nationality of the person they are calling about;
  • any advice or signposting given to them; and if going to the Home Office, which police force the information was shared with.

Any information relating to cases that have been referred to the Home Office or police will be retained by the NSPCC for 15 years.

6.0

WHERE MAY NSPCC STORE MY INFORMATION?

The NSPCC may for financial or technical reasons from time to time need to use the services of an Internet Services Provider located outside of the European Economic Area. However, NSPCC shall ensure that your personal information will be held by those Internet Service Providers in compliance with European data protection regulations. By submitting your personal information, you agree to this transfer, storing or processing at a location outside the European Economic Area. NSPCC will take all steps reasonably necessary to ensure that your data is treated securely and in accordance with this privacy policy.

7.0

HOW CAN YOU ACCESS INFORMATION NSPCC HOLD ON YOU?

You may request a copy of any personal information that the NSPCC has about you. Please write to the Data Protection Manager, NSPCC, Weston House, 42 Curtain Rd, London EC2A 3NH. The NSPCC does not make a charge for supplying this information and will reply within 40 days of receipt of your request. It will help us to locate your records more easily if you can tell us something about the nature of your contact with the NSPCC.

NSPCC want to make sure that your personal information is accurate and up to date. You may ask us to correct or remove information you think is inaccurate.
