WHAT IS A COOKIE?

This website is funded by the Home Office. It is operated, managed and hosted by the NSPCC.

This website puts small files (known as ‘cookies’) onto your computer to collect information about how you browse the website.

Cookies are used to:

  • measure how you use the website so it can be updated and improved based on your needs
  • remember the notifications you’ve seen so that we don’t show them to you again

Cookies aren’t used to identify you personally.

You’ll normally see a message on the website before a cookie is stored on your computer.

Cookies used on this website

Third parties are used to serve adverts across the Internet which drive traffic to www.modernslavery.co.uk. The cookies accompanying the ads allow monitoring of the effectiveness of these and make the ads more relevant to you. They are used for re-targeting, optimisation, reporting and attribution of online adverts.

When you visit this website you may also notice some cookies that are not related to the NSPCC or the Home Office. When you view content embedded from, for example YouTube, you may receive cookies from these websites. This website does not control the dissemination of these cookies.

How to control or delete Cookies

If you do not want cookies to be stored on your PC it is possible to disable this function within your browser without affecting your navigation around the website.

In Internet Explorer, click on Tools, click on Settings, click on the Privacy tab and then use the slider bar to choose the level of privacy you want.

In Firefox, click on Tools, click on Options, click on Privacy, click on the Cookies tab and choose your level of privacy.

For other browsers visit allaboutcookies.org

However, if you use your browser settings to block all cookies (including essential cookies) you may not be able to access all parts of the website. Unless you have adjusted your browser setting so that it will refuse cookies, the system will issue cookies as soon as you visit the website.
